Every child develops at their own pace, but understanding the general stages of development can help parents provide the right support at the right time.

## The Early Years (0-5)

During the first five years of life, children undergo remarkable physical, cognitive, and emotional growth. This is when the foundation for future learning and behavior is established.

## School Age (6-12)

As children enter school, they begin developing critical thinking skills, forming friendships, and building their identity. Parents play a crucial role in supporting academic and social development.

## Adolescence (13-18)

The teenage years bring unique challenges as children navigate identity formation, peer relationships, and increasing independence. Maintaining open communication during this stage is essential.
